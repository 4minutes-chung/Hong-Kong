### 1. Johansen rank=0 — grounds confirmed, hk_var_model.py flaw explained

Ran in TEST2.ipynb on **endogenous I(1) only**: unemployment, cpi_inflation, hk_property_price_idx.

| Test | Stats | 95% CV | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trace | [24.57, 9.56, 0.75] | [29.8, 15.49, 3.84] | rank=0 |
| Max-eigenvalue | [15.0, 8.82, 0.75] | [21.13, 14.26, 3.84] | rank=0 |

**Why hk_var_model.py gave rank=1:**
It ran Johansen on all 7 variables including us_ffr (exogenous, I(0)) and china_gdp (exogenous, I(0)).
- I(0) variables must never enter Johansen — they are already stationary and will spuriously appear to "cointegrate" with I(1) variables
- Exogenous variables by definition don't adjust to equilibrium — they can't hold a cointegrating relationship
- HIBOR-FFR moving together = LERS peg mechanics = institutional transmission, NOT equilibrium cointegration
- Result: spurious rank=1 with FFR in the cointegrating vector

**The correct procedure:** `statsmodels.tsa.vector_ar.vecm.coint_johansen` on I(1) endogenous subset only. Never use hk_var_model.py Johansen output.
