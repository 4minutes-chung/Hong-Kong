1. ESTIMAND AND DESIGN
   Estimand: Dynamic causal effects of US monetary policy shocks (us_ffr)
   and China growth shocks (china_gdp) on HK domestic variables
   (gdp_growth, cpi_inflation, unemployment, hibor_3m).
   Design: Reduced-form VAR with recursive (Cholesky) identification.
   Target coefficients: FEVD shares at h=1,4,8 quarters + IRF paths.
   Identifying variation: Time-series variation in external shocks,
   ordered before domestic variables (block-exogeneity assumption).

2. IDENTIFICATION ASSUMPTIONS
   (a) External block exogeneity: US FFR and China GDP do not respond
       contemporaneously to HK domestic shocks. STRONG and defensible
       for a small open economy. HK is <3% of China's GDP and has no
       influence on Fed policy.
   (b) Recursive ordering within domestic block: gdp_growth ordered
       before cpi_inflation, unemployment, hibor_3m. This implies
       GDP does not respond within-quarter to CPI/unemployment/HIBOR
       shocks. MODERATE — standard but debatable for HIBOR given the
       automatic currency board adjustment.
   (c) Stationarity after transforms: First-differenced I(1) variables
       (china_gdp, cpi_inflation, unemployment). Dual ADF+KPSS
       confirmed. ADEQUATE.
   (d) Time-invariant parameters: Assumes stable VAR coefficients across
       1998–2024. WEAK — this period spans the Asian Financial Crisis
       aftermath, GFC, and COVID. Sub-sample tests show mild instability
       post-GFC (max eigenvalue 1.09). LIMITATION acknowledged.

3. MODEL SPECIFICATION
   Model: VAR(3) with BIC lag selection.
     y_t = c + A_1 y_{t-1} + A_2 y_{t-2} + A_3 y_{t-3} + u_t
   K=6 variables, p=3 lags, 19 parameters per equation, 106 obs.
   Params/obs ratio: 0.18 — within acceptable range (<0.3 preferred).

   Alternative: BVAR with Minnesota prior (lambda1=0.2).
   BVAR yields qualitatively similar FEVD with tighter shrinkage.

4. DIAGNOSTICS — VERDICT
   (a) Stability: Max eigenvalue 0.993 < 1. PASS.
   (b) Residual autocorrelation: Ljung-Box rejects for china_gdp,
       cpi_inflation, unemployment at lag=8. CONCERN — suggests
       the VAR(3) does not fully capture dynamics in differenced
       variables. Higher lag order or BVAR shrinkage may help.
   (c) Cointegration: Johansen finds 1 cointegrating relationship
       among I(1) variables. A VECM would be theoretically preferred
       for long-horizon forecasts. LIMITATION — current setup
       differences away the long-run relationship.
   (d) Granger causality: china_gdp Granger-causes all 4 domestic
       variables (p < 0.005). us_ffr Granger-causes unemployment
       and hibor_3m (p < 0.005) but NOT gdp_growth or cpi_inflation
       at 5%. This is CONSISTENT with FEVD results — China acts
       through real channels, US through interest rates only.

5. MAIN RESULTS — ASSESSMENT

   FEVD at h=8 quarters (VAR, BIC p=3):
     china_gdp -> gdp_growth    : 77.5%  (BVAR: 59.7%)
     china_gdp -> cpi_inflation : 28.7%  (BVAR: 7.8%)
     china_gdp -> unemployment  : 26.8%  (BVAR: 6.7%)
     us_ffr    -> hibor_3m      : 59.2%  (BVAR: 75.2%)

   Interpretation: The finding is economically coherent. Under the
   currency board, HK imports US monetary policy mechanically (HIBOR
   tracks FFR). Meanwhile, real economic activity is driven by China
   through trade, tourism, and financial linkages. The sharp separation
   of channels is the paper's main contribution.

   Credibility check: The direction and magnitude are consistent with:
   - HK exports to China: ~55% of re-exports
   - Currency board passthrough: HIBOR correlation with FFR > 0.95
   - Literature: Genberg & He (2009) find similar qualitative patterns

6. THREATS TO VALIDITY

   (a) Data quality: World Bank annual data interpolated to quarterly
       via cubic spline. This SMOOTHS short-run variation, potentially
       biasing FEVD toward lower-frequency sources (China growth is
       slow-moving vs FFR which has genuine quarterly variation).
       SEVERITY: MEDIUM-HIGH. Fix: use official C&SD quarterly data.

   (b) HIBOR proxy: FFR + random spread instead of actual HIBOR data.
       The proxy is mechanically correlated with FFR by construction,
       which INFLATES the us_ffr -> hibor_3m FEVD share.
       SEVERITY: HIGH for HIBOR results specifically. Fix: source
       actual HIBOR from HKMA.

   (c) Cointegration ignored: Differencing I(1) variables discards
       the long-run equilibrium relationship detected by Johansen.
       This may bias long-horizon forecasts and FEVD at h=8+.
       SEVERITY: MEDIUM. Fix: implement VECM as alternative.

   (d) Residual autocorrelation in 3 of 6 equations: Suggests model
       misspecification or omitted dynamics. May affect bootstrap CI
       coverage.
       SEVERITY: MEDIUM. Fix: increase lags (but watch
       overparameterization) or add exogenous variables.

   (e) Parameter instability: Sub-sample analysis shows post-GFC
       coefficient norms diverge significantly (12.2 vs 9.2 full sample).
       Time-varying parameter VAR would be more appropriate.
       SEVERITY: LOW-MEDIUM. Acknowledged as limitation.

7. OVERALL VERDICT

   Strengths:
   + Correct econometric pipeline (stationarity -> cointegration ->
     lag selection -> estimation -> FEVD -> historical decomp -> backtest)
   + 16 mathematical audit issues identified and fixed
   + 60 unit tests passing
   + Both VAR and BVAR specifications compared
   + Robustness across 3 Cholesky orderings and 4 sub-samples
   + Research question is clear and the FEVD answers it directly

   Weaknesses:
   - Interpolated annual data (biggest issue for publication)
   - HIBOR proxy inflates the US FFR -> HIBOR channel by construction
   - Cointegration present but not modelled (should use VECM)
   - Residual autocorrelation in 3 equations not fully resolved
   - No structural identification beyond Cholesky

   Publication readiness:
   - Working paper / thesis chapter: READY with current data
   - Submission to journal: NEEDS real quarterly data from C&SD + HKMA
   - Replication package: READY (code + tests + methods note complete)

   Grade: B+ (strong execution, data limitations prevent A)
