\subsection{Identification}
\label{sec:identification}

We identify structural shocks via a recursive (Cholesky) decomposition. The
ordering---HIBOR, exports, property, GDP, CPI, unemployment---reflects
institutional timing assumptions about contemporaneous responses within a
quarter. HIBOR is ordered first: under the Linked Exchange Rate System (LERS)
currency board, the three-month interbank rate is mechanically tied to the US
federal funds rate and cannot respond to domestic output or property conditions
within the same quarter. Exports to China are ordered second: Hong Kong's
external trade flows are driven by demand originating in mainland China and are
contemporaneously exogenous to domestic price and output conditions. Property
prices are ordered third: financial asset prices adjust within the quarter to
changes in credit costs and collateral valuations \citep{BernankeGertler1989},
but aggregate output responds with a lag through investment, construction, and
household wealth effects. GDP is ordered fourth, absorbing within-quarter
responses to all three preceding variables. CPI and unemployment are ordered
last, reflecting standard price and labour market sluggishness. This ordering
does not require the full peg mechanism---only that HIBOR moves faster than
property, property faster than GDP, within a quarter. We show in
Section~\ref{sec:lp} that local projection estimates, which do not impose this
ordering, produce qualitatively consistent results at the key horizons.
