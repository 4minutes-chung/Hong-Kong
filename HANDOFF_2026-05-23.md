## What This Project Actually Is

**Research question:** How does US monetary policy and China's economic growth transmit to Hong Kong's domestic economy under the currency board (LERS)?

**Three channels being tested:**
1. **LERS channel:** US FFR → HK HIBOR (rate peg)
2. **Trade channel:** China GDP → HK exports → HK GDP
3. **Rate-property channel:** HIBOR → HK property prices

**What the model is:** A Vector Autoregression with Exogenous variables (VARX). "Exogenous" means HK cannot move US rates or China GDP — they come in from outside the system. HK responds to them.

**What it is NOT:** A causal identification strategy. This is reduced-form. The channels are economically motivated but not IV-identified. This is appropriate for a portfolio piece.

---

## Final Model Specification

```
BVAR(4) with ML-optimized Minnesota prior

Prior:      pi1=0.085, pi2=1.0, pi3=1.0
Exogenous:  us_ffr, china_gdp
Endogenous: hk_exports_china_yoy, gdp_growth, cpi_inflation,
            unemployment, hibor_3m, hk_property_price_qoq
Data:       data/hk_macro_varx_ready.csv (112 rows, 8 columns)
Sample:     1999 Q2 – 2026 Q1 (108 effective observations after 4 lags)
k:          27 parameters per equation
CI:         90% posterior credibility bands (Gibbs sampler, 2000 draws)

Cholesky ordering:
  exports → gdp → cpi → unemployment → hibor → property
```

VARX(1) is the **frequentist baseline** (the comparison point). BVAR(4) is the **preferred spec**.

---

## Why This Model and Why This Stopped Here

### Why VARX(1) not VARX(4)?
OLS at p=4 with T=111 gives 33 parameters per equation. Ratio = 3.2:1 (obs:param). At that ratio, OLS memorises in-sample noise. Proof: the exports→GDP channel completely disappears at all horizons under VARX(4)+dcpi. The model fits better statistically but kills economic signal. BIC penalty = +1.65 over VARX(1). TEST.ipynb cell 37.

### Why BVAR(4) not VARX(1)?
VARX(1) uses only one lag — misses quarterly business cycle dynamics. BVAR(4) uses 4 lags but Bayesian shrinkage (Minnesota prior) prevents variance explosion. Under BVAR(4), the exports→GDP channel is significant at h=2 (CI: +0.28, +0.54) while VARX(1) crosses zero. BVAR also wins 17/18 out-of-sample forecast cells.

### Why stop here?
The three channels are confirmed with significance and robustness:
- FEVD 18% (exports share in GDP variance) is invariant across VARX(1), BVAR(4) baseline, and BVAR(4) optimized. Invariant findings are the most defensible.
- OOS RMSE ratio < 1 on 17/18 cells = empirical out-of-sample validation.
- The acknowledged failures (2/6 Ljung-Box) are formally explained by Chow-test structural breaks at GFC 2008 and COVID 2020, not by model misspecification.

What you'd need for further research: TVP-VAR (time-varying parameters), Markov-switching VAR, Romer-Romer clean monetary shocks, or post-2014 financial channel data. None of those are in scope for a portfolio piece at this sample size.

## What Is NOT Correct / Honest Limitations

1. **2/6 Ljung-Box failures persist.** gdp_growth and cpi_inflation residuals have autocorrelation in every model tested. This is formally attributed to structural breaks (Chow test), not misspecification — but a referee would still flag it.

2. **us_ffr is a monetary-conditions level, not a policy shock.** The Romer-Romer shock series (`data/us_mp_shock_quarterly.csv`) exists and is the cleaner instrument. Not incorporated because: HK is a price-taker on US rates via LERS, so the level framing is economically defensible. But it's a limitation.

3. **hk_exports_china_yoy is nominal.** No quarterly HK export price deflator at this frequency. The YoY growth rate partially cancels slow price trends but doesn't eliminate them.

4. **No financial channel variable before 1998.** BIS Locational Banking Statistics suppress the China counterparty series pre-2014. The Stock Connect era (post-2014) and mainland property buyer channel are unmodelled.

5. **Fixed-coefficient linear model.** The structural breaks at 2008 and 2020 mean OLS parameters are averaged over regimes. The correct model is TVP-VAR or Markov-switching VAR. This is the right direction if you extend the project.

6. **HIBOR→property FEVD drops from 24% to 6–8% under BVAR(4).** More lags attribute property persistence to own history rather than contemporaneous rate movements. The contemporaneous channel is real (Cholesky h=1 is always significant) but the FEVD share is spec-dependent.

## If You Come Back to This Project

**To extend:** The most legitimate next step is TVP-VAR — it directly addresses the structural break problem by allowing parameters to vary over time. `statsmodels` does not have it; you'd need `dynare`, `pyflux`, or a custom Gibbs sampler.

**To write the research note:** Everything is in the notebook. The story is four paragraphs:
1. VARX(1) baseline — LERS and trade channel confirmed at exog coefficients
2. VARX(4) failure — OLS at p=4 kills signal (data constraint, not wrong spec)
3. Structural break proof — Chow test at 2008 and 2020; LB failures are regime breaks
4. BVAR(4) — shrinkage recovers trade channel at h=2; OOS validation confirms

**To migrate to .py files:** Start with `hk_var_model.py` already in the project. The BVAR code from TEST.ipynb cells 43–48 and the OOS loop from cell 56 are the main blocks to extract.

**To verify correctness independently:** Run the code with `MPLCONFIGDIR=/tmp/mpl_cfg python -c "..."` on key cells and compare numbers to this document. Numbers that should be stable: us_ffr→hibor coef (+0.530), china_gdp→exports coef (+0.806), exports FEVD share in GDP (18%).
