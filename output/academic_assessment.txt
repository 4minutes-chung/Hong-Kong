================================================================================
  ACADEMIC MENTOR ASSESSMENT
  Hong Kong Quarterly VAR Macro Forecasting Model
================================================================================

1. NOVELTY — adequate
   The model itself (reduced-form VAR for a small open economy) is not novel —
   it follows established practice (Lütkepohl 2005, Stock & Watson 2001).
   What adds value:
     - HK-specific calibration under the currency board constraint
     - Dual ADF+KPSS stationarity protocol with conflict resolution
     - Minnesota-prior BVAR option with auto-switching
     - Level-space scenario framework with transform inversion
   These are solid engineering contributions but not publishable as standalone
   methodology. Could serve as a **technical appendix** or **replication
   package** for a paper whose contribution lies elsewhere (e.g., HK monetary
   transmission under the peg, external shock propagation to HK).

2. FEASIBILITY — strong
   The model runs end-to-end on synthetic data and is structured to accept
   real data. The 6-variable quarterly system is well-sized for T~100.
   All diagnostics (stationarity, cointegration, Granger, stability, residual
   autocorrelation) are automated. The backtesting framework provides
   honest out-of-sample evaluation.

3. IMPACT — adequate
   For policy institutions (HKMA, finance bureau), this is a useful starting
   point. For academic publication, the impact depends on what question the
   model is used to answer. The model alone is a tool, not a finding.
   Potential high-impact applications:
     a) "How does US monetary tightening transmit to HK under the peg?"
        (use the VAR to quantify impulse-response and variance decomposition)
     b) "Does China growth matter more than US rates for HK GDP?"
        (use Granger causality + FEVD to decompose)
     c) "How accurate are HK macro forecasts during regime stress?"
        (use sub-sample backtesting across GFC/COVID)

4. METHODOLOGY — strong (post-audit)
   After the 12-issue audit fix:
     - Stationarity testing is dual-confirmed (ADF+KPSS)
     - Cointegration is checked before differencing (Johansen)
     - IRFs are Cholesky-orthogonalized consistently
     - Forecasts are correctly in level space
     - Scenario shocks are unit-consistent
     - BVAR uses proper Minnesota prior structure
   Remaining methodological gaps for publication:
     - No structural identification beyond Cholesky ordering
     - No FEVD or historical decomposition
     - No sub-sample stability analysis
     - Bootstrap does not account for parameter uncertainty
     - Cholesky ordering not robustness-tested

5. POSITIONING — where this fits

   As a standalone paper: NOT READY. Needs a research question.

   As a methods/tools paper: POSSIBLE for a practitioner journal
   (e.g., Journal of Forecasting, International Journal of Forecasting)
   if paired with real HK data and genuine forecast evaluation.

   As a chapter in a thesis on HK macro: STRONG foundation.
   Add structural identification + real data + policy question.

   As a replication/data package: READY for deposit on GitHub/Zenodo
   with a README and methods note.

6. PRIORITISED NEXT STEPS

   (a) Get real data in. The synthetic calibration is defensible for
       development but no referee will accept it for results.

   (b) Pick a research question. The model can answer several — but
       trying to answer all of them weakens each one. Choose ONE:
         - Monetary transmission under the peg
         - External shock decomposition
         - Forecast accuracy benchmarking

   (c) Add FEVD and historical decomposition. These are the outputs
       that generate interpretable economic narratives from the VAR.

   (d) Robustness: vary Cholesky ordering, sub-sample splits, compare
       VAR vs BVAR vs factor model.

   (e) Write it up: Introduction (question + contribution), Literature
       (position vs existing HK macro work), Data + Model, Results
       (IRF + FEVD + forecast evaluation), Conclusion.
